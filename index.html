How to Build a Survivor Shelter

The first thing you want to do when you get to your new Survivor tribe camp is to build a nice bamboo shelter for you and your tribe. With a bamboo shelter, you and your tribe can relax in your camp while you're waiting for the next Survivor challenge or Tribal Council, and you'll be protected from the harsh weather and jungle elements that may take a big impact on you and your tribe at your camp. Now here are the steps into buiding a nice Survivor shelter:

1.

Design the Construction for Your Shelter
  • A typical Survivor shelter would be simple and practical.
  • The size of the shelter should be enough to meet your needs.
  • My recommendation is try to stick to a simple size Survivor shelter

2.

Gather the Materials for Your Shelter
  • Gather sturdy bamboo from the jungle with the machete to chop them down.
  • Use the machete to gather enough palm fronds for the roof of your shelter.
  • Try to find driftwood near the ocean shore of your camp.
* Note that if you lose the marooning challenge on the 1st day of Survivor, you might need to wait until after the 1st immunity challenge to get the machete if you tribe fails in your 2nd attempt at earning your supplies. Until then, you'll just have to gather them all by hand.

3.

Build the Structure of Your Shelter
  • Stabilize your base by digging down to firmer ground or use rocks.
  • Lay out the bamboo poles for you ground base, roof, beams, and posts.
  • Try to build the structure in an "A-Frame" design.

4.

Make Walls and Roofing in Your Shelter
  • Interwieve the palm fronds for the walls to create a mesh that will shield from the wind.
  • Lay out the other palm fronds on the roof of your shelter with overlapping layers to ensure it's waterproof to prevent any leaks in the shelter.

5.

Make Your Shelter Sturdy
  • Use woven bamboo or bamboo splits to seal the grids and make your shelter as strong as possible.
* Note that if your tribe has a Survivor tool kit, you may use the tools inside to make your shelter as strong as possible.

6.

Try To Enhance Your Shelter as Much as You Can
  • Use sand to keep the interior cool during your time on Survivor
  • Use palm fronds or leaves as blankets to keep yourself warm at night.
  • If you tribe wins a tarp, use it to protect yourself from the rain and sun's heat.
  • If you tribe win a hammock, then try to take a nap on it if you have the time., if you tribe doesn't, then you'll have to wait until the merge to use it.
Now here's some stuff to keep in mind for your Survivor shelter:
  • Make sure that your shelter is properly secured.
  • Regularly check it for damage and necessary repairs
  • Make sure it's resistant to animals out in the jungle.
  • Try to reinforce the lower parts of the walls or ensure that food securly stored, away from the sleeping areas of animals.
  • Be careful if you're sleeping on the bamboo floor because it's hard, and you can accidently disable your radial nerve by having it sleep on it for too long.
